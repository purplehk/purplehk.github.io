Reflection

About

My final project is to build a portfolio website for my small business marketing consultancy. I started with something in the previous weeks, but during the time we took to build our partner's websites, I realized I want to go in a different direction with my site layout. I've seen many different corporate B2B sites, especially those done by agencies/creative firms. Some of the specific ones I can think of off the top of my head belong to companies I used to work for such as www.360i.com and www.fluidhk.com. I've also done a fair amount of looking around Themeforest because I used to use pre-made themes to create websites for my clients. I think my brain remembers little things here and there of what I like, and I try to piece it all together.

Strengths

  • General sense of user experience - I think I have a good sense of ease of usability, though I do want to work more on focal point and general flow
  • Simple, clean design - I've always been a fan of websites with simple, clean design, so I'm happy to implement it in my site
  • Attention to detail - I try to make sure that things are lined up, or hover states or colors are matching, etc.

Struggles

  • Typography - I'm not well-versed in Typography, figuring out what works with what, so it always takes me a lot longer to find typefaces and font styles I like
  • General Style - I want my site to have character, not to be too generic, but at the same time not so strange that it suffers in usability

Review

Business of Fashion

  • Layout - two column, clear header
  • Typography - consistent serif typeface for headers and paragraph with a sans serif for the byline, captions are italicized for emphasis
  • Navigation - horizontal navigation, very clear across the top
  • Overall Flow - Z-layout, clear focal points on the logo, nav and hero image
  • Other Notes - use of colors to denote content category, Chinese site has a different layout because Chinese text can look very cluttered

Spectrum Powderworks

  • Layout - single column
  • Typography - same sans serif font for logo and navigation with a different sans serif font when hovering over images, colors of navigation make it not very noticeable, logo font makes it seem a bit spooky, unreliable, unprofessional + red is a bit intense, the repeat watermark of the logo is also very strange
  • Navigation - seems to be too many navigation options: the images themselves and then the bottom navigation
  • Overall Flow - unclear where the focal point is: at first I want to use the bottom navigation because it is the most obvious, but then I see that there are more links when hovering over the images
  • Other Notes - hard to understand what the image behind the logo text is, too much animation is distracting
